Thinkering

Lessons we learned through consultation and reading

Cultural Respect Through Built Form


Cedar should be used as much as possible

Can we plant a cedar forest for future generations to enjoy?

References to weaving, earthtones with splashes of ochre and purple should figure prominently

Wayfinding should honour the language by using geographical references: Forestside, Upriver, Downriver, and etc.

Consider durational public art, begun now for future generations

Design and include a symbolic nail at the entrance, or all entrances, on which to hang your worries and incongruent thoughts

Welcome posts are a given

Design and build an outdoor fitness circuit, based on warrior training from Musqueam culture

Attract birds and other wildlife

As possible, scatter amenities throughout each building instead of concentrating them all on one floor, to encourage use of the whole building as your home

Water should be always visible, streams and sluices that could lead to a central gathering place

The river shoreline should be represented where edges between landscape materials are needed: perhaps we even ban straight lines?

River grass should be planted in abundance

Plantings rescued before site clearing should be nurtured off-site, and brought back to the site when landscaping commences

Consider light-bulbs that use the same wavelengths as sunlight

Design and install a memorial place, for anyone who needs a quiet spot to remember their ancestors or lost ones, regardless of culture

Find unique ways to represent connection: could all buildings have a steel bar that connects them physically, like a spider web that touches each structure in the community?

In community spaces and perhaps even in private homes, moveable walls, especially if they reference woven blankets in some way, would symbolically represent the blankets used to create rooms in the longhouse


Cultural Respect Through Belonging


An App or community website could be developed where skills are shared and goods are exchanged, or given away

Programs to connect youth and elders should be developed

Elders can teach and youth could be trained to be community ambassadors

A big-brother/big-sister/volunteer-grandparents program could be implemented

Seniors and mothers with children will appreciate ample lighting, and more seating areas than expected

Seating can be physically designed and connected, to encourage interaction

Mailboxes can be lowered to encourage conversation

Other devices should be thought about that will make connecting with others more likely

Consider references to cultural themes in the way bylaws are written. Perhaps they include a social contract, where you must give back to the community in some way


Cultural Respect Through Marketing Tactics


As MCC is a business to business institution, and while MIB addresses the world, consider an outward-facing MIB website, and transferring inward-facing language to the MCC website, or a site specifically accessible only by MIB members

The Block F logo should relate to, and be born of, the MIB logo

Stories should be everywhere: a highly-visible and recognizable symbol to indicate “There is a Story Here,” should be created that can be part of the landscape, building details, paving stones, and all other places where it could potentially be used

A permanent recording device could be installed in the community centre, where people can record their stories, and answer the question “Who are you and where are you from?” These should be accessed easily by anyone interested in listening
