Key Insights

According to our own survey research, and our overview of existing studies:

Canadian culture is Indigenous culture:


9 / 10 Canadians acknowledge Indigenous history and culture as important characteristics of Canadaâ€™s identity as a country.


People are curious:


Across Canada, 8 / 10 strongly (39%) or somewhat (40%) agree that they would be personally interested in learning more about Aboriginal cultures; interest is particularly strong in B.C. (46% strongly agree).


There are many shared values:


Many British Columbians hold what they perceive to be Indigenous values, in high regard. These include:

Conservation & sustainability

Traditional knowledge

Commitment to spiritual life, religion, and health

Respect for the land and the elders


Musqueam culture has themes that are highly-valued:


Cultural themes from our consultation process were rank ordered by survey respondents as follows:

Family & friendship

Community

Belonging & togetherness

Sharing & education


We have an added value-advantage:


Interest in a community based on Musqueam cultural values is attractive (or neutral) amongst BC respondents: 42% neutral, 18% somewhat attractive, 8% extremely attractive.


Conclusion:


Our consultation process with Musqueam community members taught us that how we treat others reflects back on ourselves, and contributes to our own success.

We would interpret the research findings as a strong indication that people in our area are hungry for what we can offer. We should feed them, offer what we have, so all can get to a good place in a good way.
